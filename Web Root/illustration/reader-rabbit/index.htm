The Reader Rabbit Project

2003 Hired as a contractor by Publicom Inc. to convert content from computer games into print medium for a pre-K to first grade audience. My roles included illustration, page layout, content modification, and visual standards enforcement. Our team built three workbooks (each with 300 pages of educational activities) in twelve weeks, either meeting or exceeding our client expectations.

Spot Illustration

Developing approved thumbnail sketches into finished vector art, sometimes responding to new art direction on the fly while staying on model under tight deadlines.

Trademarked Characters

The wide variety of styles used on previous Reader Rabbit art made this task a challenge. The product packaging did not have a consistent look, and art from the CD ROMs was too low in resolution for use in print. Therefore, concocting a style for this project came by trial-and-error (as well as a lot of patience from everyone involved!)

Duties
Rendered final art for various spot illustrations
Chosen to render "on-spec" versions of trademarked characters (Reader Rabbit, Sam the Lion)
All art rendered as vector files (Adobe Illustrator)
Corrected or redrew final art created by outside illustrators

Page Layout

Each designer was given an entire chapter to build. The "shell" is actual production size, complete with the approved font treatment. My job was to apply text content, then design the page visually.

During this early phase of the design process, specific illustrators weren't yet assigned to specific chapters. Therefore, I felt that making my thumbnail sketches as detailed as time permitted would only help the illustrators.

This is the previous page, as rendered by an outside illustrator. The illustrator's task was to produce child-friendly, Disney-like finished art. My duty, as one of the more illustrative Page Designers on the team, was to make my place-holding sketches detailed enough for the artists to do their magic.

Duties
Applied new content to new design "shell" pages
Produced live-sized "thumbnails" of entire chapters
Created "thumbnails" of spot art for size, content and placement
Ensured and developed the clarity and workability of each exercise
Offered alternatives to exercises that didn't work

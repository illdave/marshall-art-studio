Comic Book Art and Design

I've been designing and producing my printed comics since 2012, specializing in mimicking old school visual effects with modern digital tools. Elements include cover and page design, creating logos (usually by hand with traditional tools), vetting printers, post-production art corrections, and working closely with printers throughout the production process.

Cover Design

My covers are designed to reflect different historical periods of comics as related to the stories. Elements include specific design shells, logo, cover blurb and paper treatments. Learn more.

Page Design

The historical referencing from the covers is extended to the interior pages. Examples include letters pages, faux ads, and thematic one-pagers. Learn more.

Logos

Bringing my pre-digital production skills to the modern era. Logos designed by hand with traditional media (technical pens, French curves, vellum paper, Pro-White, etc.) Learn more.

Production

Using the same traditional print media dynamics from the early days of my career to emulate the look-and-feel of Silver-Bronze Age comics. Learn more.

My Comic Book Work

The art, lettering, and logos are done in traditional ink-on-paper media. Most original art is 12 x 18 inches. I'm currently obsessed with using modern digital tools to mimic old school print techniques.

Inky Stories

Anthology of my work, self-published by Rotten Kid Press. Selected stories are free to read as web comics. Printed books on the online store. Learn more.

Commissions

I will make custom, comic book-related art for you. Sensibly priced by size, deadline, and complexity. Learn more.

Art of the Comic Book

Studio workshop for making comic book stories with ink-on-paper media. Emphasis on storytelling, collaboration, and more. Learn more.
